## Caption

Your safari camera settings cheat sheet is here. Save this post before your trip - these 5 settings are the difference between blurry disappointment and frame-worthy wildlife shots.

After 12+ years guiding photographers through Kenya and Tanzania, our team at Simbali Wild Safaris knows exactly what works in the bush. The most common mistake? Using auto mode and hoping for the best. Wildlife does not pose - you need your settings dialed in before the moment happens.

Quick version: 1/1000s shutter speed to freeze motion, f/5.6 aperture for creamy background blur, ISO 400-800 in daylight, continuous autofocus to track moving animals, and RAW for editing flexibility.

Our safari vehicles are designed with photographers in mind, and our driver-guides understand light angles and animal behavior. Whether you are shooting with a DSLR, mirrorless camera, bridge camera, or smartphone, we will help place you in position for extraordinary shots.
